WTH is Horror?

Merriam-Webster defines horror as : A painful and intense, fear, dread, or dismay.
That sounds horrible, but when used as a vessel for storytelling it can do a whole lot more than just scare you.
So let me officailly welcome you to my Ted Talk. Learning through horror, or alternatively How I learned to love the scare.

Horro's Reach

Name's such as, Michael Myers, PennyWise, Krueger, The Scream evoke a feeling that can be summed up as "Yeah I don't want to run into that guy."
Now you'll say that these are all just fictional, they don't extend to real life. Is that true though?
An abandoned house with a history of unfortunate events, a portrait that seems to follow your every move.
That lonely road surrounded by trees you won't ever take cause it reminds you of that one scene.
These are examples of the reach that horror as a genre has outside of it's respective mediums.
It turns the mundane into the extraordiany, but it doesn't just end at loss of hope.
If anything it leads us to one important super important point coming up. Triumph.

F.E.A.R

Now let's talk about F.E.A.R an acronym that has two definition's in regards to horror.
There is the typical response definition Forget Everythings And Run, which is what we have when faced with what seems like an unwinnable situation.
I mean you see something like a Xenomporh from Alien's running at you, there is no other response outside of getting the hell out of dodge and there is nothing wrong with that.
However even the scared has to turn towards the threat and say enough is enough. This is the point where the other definition comes in, Face Everything And Rise.
What the Xenomporh, Freddy, Pennywise, and ther est have in common is that they were eventually defeated by the ones who were afraid of them.

The Lesson Learned

This was just a simple set up to talk about my favorite genre Horror, and what it can do beyond just giving you a scare. Horror teaches that no matter what the monster is there is a time to run away, but eventually you have to face it. And it's the same as programming, the idea of making something as simple as a barebones website feel like a daunting task. JavaScript, CSS, HTML, and the variables included are nothing more than the typical horror movie villains. And just the same as those villains, they can be conquered.
